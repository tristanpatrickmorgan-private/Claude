# Training Plan

Beginner-friendly plan — basic cardio + upper body strength.
2–3 sessions per week, 45 min each, all at home.

## Equipment
- Treadmill
- Barbell with plates (8–32 kg total)
- Kettlebells 6, 12, 20 kg
- Step
- Resistance bands

## Week structure
- **2x/week:** Session A + Session B
- **3x/week:** Session A + Session B + Session C

Try to leave at least one rest day between strength sessions.

---

## Session A — Push + Cardio (45 min)

**Warm-up (5 min)** — easy treadmill walk, then light jog.

**Strength (~20 min)** — 3 rounds, rest 60–90s between rounds:
- Barbell floor press (no rack needed — lie on floor): 5–8 reps
- Kettlebell goblet squat (start 12 kg): 10 reps
- Barbell overhead press (standing): 8 reps
- Plank: 20–30s

**Cardio (~15 min)** — treadmill intervals: 2 min easy / 1 min faster, repeat.

**Cooldown (5 min)** — stretch chest, shoulders, hips.

---

## Session B — Pull + Cardio (45 min)

**Warm-up (5 min)** — easy treadmill + band pull-aparts.

**Strength (~20 min)** — 3 rounds, rest 60–90s:
- Barbell bent-over row: 8–10 reps
- Resistance band pull-apart: 12 reps
- Barbell curl: 10 reps
- Step-ups onto the step: 10 each leg
- Dead bug or hollow hold: 20–30s

**Cardio (~15 min)** — steady moderate pace on treadmill.

**Cooldown (5 min)** — stretch back, hamstrings, calves.

---

## Session C — Cardio + Mobility (45 min, optional 3rd day)

- 5 min walk warm-up
- 25–30 min easy run/walk intervals (whatever feels manageable)
- 10 min stretching with bands — full body

---

## Progression rules
- Start with a weight that's challenging by the last 1–2 reps, not the first.
- When you can finish all 3 rounds with good form for 2 sessions in a row, add reps or move up in weight.
- If form breaks down, drop the weight.

## Getting started
- First 2–3 weeks: focus on form, don't chase weight. It will feel almost too easy.
- Some soreness is normal. Sharp or joint pain is not — stop.
- Missed a session? Just pick up next time. Consistency beats intensity.

---

## Exercise Guide

### Barbell floor press
Lie on your back on the floor with the barbell on the ground beside you. Roll it over your chest, grip slightly wider than shoulder width with palms facing your feet. Press up until arms are straight, lower until your upper arms rest on the floor, then press again. Keep your wrists straight — not bent back. The floor limits your range of motion which is actually safer when learning. Start very light (just the bar) to get the movement right.

### Barbell overhead press
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Hold the bar at upper chest height, grip just outside your shoulders. Press the bar straight up overhead until arms are fully extended. As the bar passes your face, move your head back slightly, then bring it forward again once the bar is clear. Lower back to upper chest. Keep your core tight and don't lean back — if you're arching your lower back, the weight is too heavy.

### Kettlebell goblet squat
Hold the kettlebell with both hands at chest height, gripping the sides of the handle (like holding a goblet). Feet shoulder-width, toes turned out slightly. Push your hips back and bend your knees, lowering until thighs are parallel to the floor or as far as is comfortable. Keep your chest up and knees tracking over your toes — don't let them cave inward. Drive through your heels to stand back up.

### Plank
Lie face down, then prop yourself up on your forearms (elbows under shoulders) and toes. Your body should form a straight line from head to heels — don't let your hips sag or stick up. Squeeze your core and glutes. Breathe normally. If 20–30s is easy, extend the time rather than the reps.

### Barbell bent-over row
Stand with feet shoulder-width, barbell on the floor or held at thigh height. Hinge forward at the hips until your torso is at roughly 45 degrees (back flat, not rounded). Let the bar hang at arm's length, then pull it into your lower chest/upper stomach, leading with your elbows. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top. Lower with control. A rounded lower back means the weight is too heavy — reduce it.

### Resistance band pull-apart
Hold the band with both hands at shoulder width, arms straight out in front of you at chest height. Keeping your arms straight, pull your hands apart and out to the sides until the band touches your chest. Slowly return to the start. Focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together as you pull. This is a small movement — don't rush it.

### Barbell curl
Stand holding the barbell with palms facing up, hands shoulder-width apart. Keep your elbows pinned to your sides throughout — they shouldn't drift forward. Curl the bar up toward your shoulders, squeeze at the top, then lower slowly (take 2–3 seconds on the way down). The slow lower is where most of the work happens. Don't swing your back to lift the weight.

### Step-ups
Stand facing the step. Place one foot fully on the step, then drive through that heel to step up until you're standing on it with both feet. Step back down and repeat. Do all reps on one leg before switching, or alternate. Keep the movement controlled — don't push off the back foot to cheat the rep. Hold a kettlebell in each hand to add difficulty when it gets easy.

### Dead bug
Lie on your back with arms pointing straight up toward the ceiling and knees bent at 90 degrees (shins parallel to the floor — "tabletop" position). Slowly lower your right arm toward the floor behind your head while at the same time straightening and lowering your left leg toward the floor. Keep your lower back pressed flat against the floor throughout — this is the whole point of the exercise. Return to start and repeat on the other side. Go slowly.

### Treadmill intervals (Session A)
After your warm-up jog, increase speed to a pace where you could still speak in short sentences but it feels like effort — not a sprint. Hold that for 1 minute, then drop back to an easy jog or fast walk for 2 minutes. Repeat this cycle for 12–15 minutes. As fitness improves, make the fast intervals slightly longer or slightly faster.

### Treadmill steady state (Session B)
A pace you could hold a conversation at — moderate effort, nothing more. This trains your aerobic base. Aim to keep the same pace for the full 15 minutes without stopping. If you need to walk, walk — don't stop. Over time this pace will feel easier, which is the goal.

### Stretching with bands (Session C)
Use the band to assist stretches you can't quite reach on your own:
- **Hamstring:** Lie on back, loop band around one foot, pull leg toward you with straight knee.
- **Chest/shoulder:** Hold band behind you with both hands, lift arms away from your back.
- **Hip flexor:** Kneel in a lunge position, loop band around the front knee and anchor it behind you for a gentle pull. Hold each stretch 30–45 seconds.
