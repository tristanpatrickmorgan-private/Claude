# Half-Scale Jeep / Land Rover Build

## Concept
Build a half-scale WW2 Willys Jeep or Series 1 Land Rover — electrically driven, for Archie to ride in. DIY build, plans to be purchased.

## Status
Early research phase — reviewing plans options.

## Best leads

### Land Rover — Series 1 (strongest options)
- [Toylander 1 — 1948 Series 1 Land Rover](https://www.toylander.com/vehicles/toylander-1) — miniature replica of original Series 1. Purchasable build manual with full cutting details and dimensions for all body panels. Also stocks parts. **Recommended starting point.**
- [Toylander Build Manual](https://www.toylander.com/build-manual-tl2) — buy the plans here
- [Rebel Replicas](https://silodrome.com/land-rover-series-i-half-scale/) — UK company (Worcestershire) making ½ scale electric Series 1, Lightweight and Defender. 6mph top speed, fits one adult or two children. Plans and kits available.
- [PistonHeads Toylander build thread](https://www.pistonheads.com/gassing/topic.asp?h=0&f=207&t=1683084) — community build discussion

### WW2 Willys Jeep MB (less commercially available, more scratch build)
- [Scratch-built ½ scale WW2 Willys Jeep — RPF forum](https://www.therpf.com/forums/threads/scratch-built-1-2-scale-ww2-willys-jeep.286811/) — detailed scratch build, no plans but excellent reference
- [Kid Electric Jeep — Instructables](https://www.instructables.com/Kid-Electric-Jeep/) — step-by-step electric Jeep build for a child
- [Mobility Scooter Kids Jeep — Instructables](https://www.instructables.com/Mobility-Scooter-Kids-Jeep/) — uses mobility scooter as donor drive system
- [Electric Mini Jeep — DIY Go Karts forum](https://www.diygokarts.com/community/threads/electric-mini-jeep.40367/) — 2x 500W motors, speed controller, working lights, 2x 35Ah batteries
- [Mini Jeep plans — DIY Go Karts forum](https://www.diygokarts.com/community/threads/mini-jeep-plans.39327/) — community discussion on sourcing plans
- [Mini Jeep Building Plans — All About Mini Bikes](https://www.allaboutminibikes.com/build-a-mini-jeep.html) — classic plans from Boy's Life magazine
