# London 2-bed central flat market — research May 2026

Snapshot for the Battersea/SW8 flat at 76a Ingelow Road. Used as input to the yield analysis.

## Headline data (12 months to Feb/Mar 2026)

London is the worst-performing UK region. Flats are leading the decline.

| Segment | Change YoY |
|---|---:|
| London overall | −3.3% |
| London flats (national stat) | −3.8% |
| **Wandsworth flats** | **−7.1%** |
| Kensington & Chelsea flats | −11.8% |
| Prime central London (£4.5m+) | −4.8% (24.5% below 2014 peak) |

- Battersea flats now average ~£537,500
- Asking prices in Battersea down 2.5% over last 6 months
- London transactions down ~20% YoY
- Time-to-sell: 60–75 days (vs ~40 in 2021)
- Average discount from initial asking: 10.8% (Feb 2026)

## Why this is happening — structural drivers

1. **Mortgage rates** — base rate 3.75% (April 2026), still well above 2021 floor. Flats most affected because leverage is highest.
2. **Non-dom abolition (April 2025)** — worldwide income tax after 4 years residence; 40% IHT on global assets after 10 years. 10,800+ millionaires left UK in 2024-25. 70% of £15m+ London homes listed by non-dom sellers. Trickled down from prime.
3. **SDLT surcharge** — second-home rate raised from 3% to **5% on 31 Oct 2024**. Buy-to-let purchases of central London flats heavily depressed.
4. **Cladding / building safety** — still unresolved a year into Labour government. EWS1 transactions stalling. Disproportionately hits central London flats in post-2000 blocks.
5. **WFH / preference shift** — post-2020 "race for space" structurally shifted demand toward houses and outer zones.
6. **Renters' Rights Act + tax burden** — small landlords exiting, increasing flat supply while reducing BTL demand.

## Outlook — consensus "bottom 2026, gradual recovery from 2027"

| Forecaster | View |
|---|---|
| **Savills** | 2026 is the bottom. **+8.1% cumulative over 5 years**. "Houses have held up better than flats and this is likely to continue." |
| **Knight Frank** | Downgraded prime central London to **−2% for 2026**. Two-speed market: built-complete OK, off-plan weak. |
| **Hamptons** | Landlords consolidating, not returning. First-time buyer competition in London is weakest in UK. |
| **Rate markets** | ~80% chance base rate at 3.25% by Nov 2026. Capital Economics & Morgan Stanley see 3.0%. |

**No analyst forecasts a sharp rebound.** Tax/regulatory headwinds are structural, not cyclical.

## Implications for 76a Ingelow Road

- Worst-hit category: London 2-bed flat in Wandsworth
- Past 12 months: −7.1% (~£56k loss on £800k value)
- Forward 12 months: flat-to-modestly-down per Savills/Knight Frank
- 5-year forecast: low single-digit cumulative growth
- Rents rising 4%/yr → cash yield improving organically
- Cash-on-cash yield ~2% on £621k equity vs ~4.5% on gilts = ~£14k/yr opportunity cost
- Without appreciation, the asset is dilutive to total wealth vs risk-free alternatives
