# 2026 — Holiday Planning

## Principles
- **Do something new and independent each year.** Family places (Gausta, Sannäs) are easy and flexible but lack novelty and the feel of a proper trip away. Sannäs in particular can be busy with family, making long stretches less relaxing. Ireland is a great template — parents' house so free and flexible, no logistics pressure — but flights are expensive and the timing hasn't worked out. The goal is at least one trip a year that's genuinely new.
- **Break up the long stretch to summer.** End of May or early June is a good slot for a long weekend trip — drivable from Gothenburg or a direct flight. Good for a young family. Hotels are fine for short stints but not ideal for longer stays — aim for self-catering or a house where possible.

## Long weekend / independent trip

**Principle:** Do something new and independent each year — not just Gausta/Sannäs. End of May or early June is a good slot to break up the stretch before summer. All options below are direct from Gothenburg Landvetter. Hotels ok for short trips but self-catering preferred. Needs to work for a young family (Archie 2, Eloise infant).

**Nap structure:** Archie naps 12–2pm; Eloise (~6 months) naps ~9am, 12pm, 4pm. Working pattern: activity 9:30–11:30am → back to base for naps 12–2pm → light afternoon activity or beach 3–4:30pm → early dinner 5:30pm → bed 7pm. Max 2 activities per day.

**Temperature limit:** Anything near 30°C becomes hard work with young kids. All windows below are filtered for this.

### Summary comparison

| | Porto | Corfu | Malta | Barcelona |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Malta is easiest and cheapest. Corfu late May with a villa is probably the best overall experience with a toddler and a baby. Porto is the most interesting city break. Barcelona is the most expensive and busiest — least naturally suited to this stage of life.

### Porto — 4 nights, late May (or September)

**20–22°C. Great value. Best city-break option.**

**Where to stay:** Bonfim or Ribeira area — walkable to sights, good for pushchairs on flatter streets.
- Hotel (family room, mid-range): ~900–1,300 SEK/night → 4 nights ~3,600–5,200 SEK
- Self-catering apartment (Bonfim, more space): ~800–1,200 SEK/night → ~3,200–4,800 SEK

**Itinerary:**
- Day 1 (arrive afternoon): Ribeira waterfront walk, dinner at a tascas by the river
- Day 2: Morning — Palácio de Cristal gardens (hilltop park, free, great for toddlers, stunning views). Afternoon — cross Dom Luís bridge to Gaia, one wine lodge tour (Sandeman or Graham's, ~€15pp, gardens for kids, 1 hour)
- Day 3: Morning — Livraria Lello (book online, ~€5pp, stunning Art Nouveau bookshop, 30–45 mins) + Torre dos Clérigos area. Afternoon — Uber to Matosinhos beach (~15 min, sandy, Archie can paddle, excellent seafood restaurants)
- Day 4: Morning — Serralves Museum and Park (huge park, children free, ~€10pp adults). Afternoon — wander Bonfim, gelato
- Day 5: Fly home

**Budget:**
| Item | Low | High |
|---|---|---|
| Flights (2 adults + child + infant, Ryanair/SAS return) | 5,000 SEK | 7,500 SEK |
| Hotel 4 nights | 3,600 SEK | 5,200 SEK |
| Food (4 days — Porto is great value) | 3,000 SEK | 4,000 SEK |
| Activities (Lello, Serralves, wine lodge) | 1,200 SEK | 1,500 SEK |
| Taxis/Ubers | 600 SEK | 900 SEK |
| **Total** | **~13,000 SEK** | **~19,000 SEK** |

### Corfu — 4 nights, late May only

**~23°C, sea 21°C. Most relaxed. Best overall with young kids.**

June already 28°C+, September 27–29°C — late May is the only suitable window.

**Where to stay:** Base in a beach village, not Corfu Town. Dassia (15 min from town, calm flat water, good for paddling) or Agios Gordios (sandy west coast beach, more rustic). A villa with a private pool is transformative with a toddler and a baby — Archie in the pool all afternoon, Eloise in the shade, no need to travel anywhere.
- Hotel (family-run, beach village): ~900–1,400 SEK/night → 4 nights ~3,600–5,600 SEK
- Villa with private pool: ~1,300–1,900 SEK/night → ~5,200–7,600 SEK *(worth the premium with 2 young kids)*

**Itinerary:**
- Day 1 (arrive afternoon): Beach, settle in, village taverna dinner
- Day 2: Morning — Corfu Town (UNESCO Venetian old town, Liston promenade — wide, flat, pushchair-friendly, ice cream). Afternoon — back to beach/pool
- Day 3: Morning — Paleokastritsa (short boat trip around the sea grottos ~€10pp, 20 mins; walk up to the monastery for the views). Afternoon — pool/beach
- Day 4: Morning — Achilleion Palace gardens (Empress Sisi's hilltop palace, open gardens, big views, Archie can roam freely). Afternoon — final beach afternoon
- Day 5: Fly home

**Budget:**
| Item | Hotel | Villa |
|---|---|---|
| Flights (Ryanair/SAS return, late May) | 6,000–8,500 SEK | 6,000–8,500 SEK |
| Accommodation 4 nights | 3,600–5,600 SEK | 5,200–7,600 SEK |
| Food (tavernas — Greece is great value) | 2,500–3,500 SEK | 2,000–3,000 SEK |
| Car hire (useful for day trips) | 1,600 SEK | 1,600 SEK |

### Malta — 3 nights, May or October

**23–25°C. Most affordable. Easiest with young kids — English everywhere.**

June onwards hits 29–33°C. Avoid summer entirely.

**Where to stay:** Sliema — flat seafront promenade (brilliant for a pushchair), 10-min ferry to Valletta, good restaurants. St George's Bay nearby has a small sandy beach.
- Hotel (Sliema, mid-range): ~1,000–1,600 SEK/night → 3 nights ~3,000–4,800 SEK
- Self-catering apartment (Sliema seafront): ~900–1,400 SEK/night → ~2,700–4,200 SEK

**Itinerary:**
- Day 1 (arrive afternoon): Sliema promenade walk, dinner
- Day 2: Morning — ferry to Valletta (~€3 return, Archie loves a boat) → Upper Barrakka Gardens (cannon salute daily at noon — Archie will love it — plus incredible Grand Harbour views). Afternoon — Sliema lido/seafront
- Day 3: Morning — Mdina, the Silent City (ancient walled hilltop city, almost no cars, medieval streets, magical and quiet, ~30 min taxi). Afternoon — Blue Grotto boat trip (20-min boat through sea caves, turquoise water, ~€10pp) or relax in Sliema
- Day 4: Fly home

**Budget:**
| Item | Low | High |
|---|---|---|
| Flights (Ryanair return — cheapest route of the four) | 4,500 SEK | 7,000 SEK |
| Hotel 3 nights | 3,000 SEK | 4,800 SEK |
| Food (3 days, reasonable, easy in English) | 2,000 SEK | 3,000 SEK |
| Ferry + activities | 800 SEK | 1,000 SEK |
| Taxis | 500 SEK | 700 SEK |
| **Total** | **~11,000 SEK** | **~16,500 SEK** |

### Barcelona — 3 nights, May only

**20–22°C. Most expensive. Most stimulating — but also most demanding with young kids.**

Late September can touch 30°C, so May is the only safe window.

**Where to stay:** Eixample — wide boulevards, easy with a pram, central, good restaurants. Avoid Gothic Quarter as a base (too narrow, too crowded with a pram).
- Hotel (Eixample, family room): ~1,500–2,200 SEK/night → 3 nights ~4,500–6,600 SEK
- Apartment (Eixample or Gràcia): ~1,200–1,800 SEK/night

**Itinerary:**
- Day 1 (arrive afternoon): Walk to Barceloneta beach, seafood dinner on the front
- Day 2: Morning — Sagrada Família (book timed entry, ~€30pp — the towers and stained glass are extraordinary, allow 1.5 hours). Afternoon — Parc de la Ciutadella (huge park, rowing lake, waterfall, free — perfect for a toddler)
- Day 3: Morning — Park Güell (book morning slot, ~€15pp for the mosaic terrace, 30 mins is enough; rest of park free and lovely). Afternoon — Barceloneta beach, paddle and sand
- Day 4: Fly home

**Budget:**
| Item | Low | High |
|---|---|---|
| Flights (Norwegian return — less frequent, pricier) | 7,000 SEK | 10,000 SEK |
| Hotel 3 nights | 4,500 SEK | 6,600 SEK |
| Food (3 days — Barcelona not cheap) | 4,000 SEK | 5,500 SEK |
| Activities (Sagrada Família + Park Güell) | 1,800 SEK | 2,000 SEK |
| Transport | 700 SEK | 1,000 SEK |
| **Total** | **~18,000 SEK** | **~25,000 SEK** |

### Full options researched (for reference)
- **Palma de Mallorca** — Ryanair/Norwegian/SAS, ~3h10m, May–Sept. Best all-round family beach, excellent infrastructure. Reliable but feels more package-holiday.
- **Faro/Algarve** — SAS direct seasonal (April–May only). ~4h. Great beaches for young kids.
- **Edinburgh** — Ryanair, ~2h, May–Sept. Great city for toddlers (castle, zoo, parks), manageable size.
- **Nice** — Norwegian/SAS, ~2h30m, Jun–Sept. French Riviera, beaches + culture, shorter flight.
- **Alicante / Málaga** — Norwegian/Ryanair, ~3h, Jun–Sept. Costa Blanca/Sol, reliable sun.
- **Amsterdam** — KLM, ~2h, May–Jun. Very family friendly city, easy logistics.
- **Reykjavik** — Icelandair, ~3h, Jun–Aug only. Dramatic and unique, but cold and no beach.
